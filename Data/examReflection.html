Reflection:

Exam Graph


For my Exam Graph i chose to use a new data set - sourced from the world population index website, population data and relate that data to the air quality that we see from the cities used throughout the website. Although for this graph i was particularly interested in examining the global south's relationship in this regard. And some of the findings were very interesting and suprising to learn.

In Blog number 6 we discussed the various factors that contribute to global warming and thus a cities air quality index, and one of the highest emission activities was, vehicles. And the larger a cities population the more vehicles potentially on the road. These two variables in my opinion have a direct correlation to each other because of this reason. The population data retrieved is not particularly live data, instead it is static. I found this reasonable to use because population data would understandably, be tricky to compute live. Hence, i have downloaded json files sourced from the world population website - in the file is past and future (predicted) population statistics from the year 1950 to 2035. I opted for this because i could not find a resource that feeds live updates of specific cities instead of the general world population (which is the only live data set i found, in relation to population)


What can be learnt from this new data set?


Its not very clear what can be learnt from this because, my initial assumption was that in the case of Lagos, because i knew that it has very high volumes of population density, but at the time that i write this reflection (because the air quality fluctuates throughout the day)Lagos's air quality index is considerably (lower) which means better than Accra's air quality which has the highest index but lowest population. So thats a major suprise to me. However, what makes sense is that Johannesburg being the most densely populated city in South Africa, currently has the worst air quality. Which makes sense because more people means more vehicles, electricity usage, and generally more footprint on the environment.

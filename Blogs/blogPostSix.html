What is Interaction For Visualisation?


Interaction has been an area of interest within fields such as games studies, Hci - community, and data visualisation. However, there has been much debate concerning the definition of interaction and its role in relation to visualisation.Because overtime different field prioritised different needs for the end user for different reasons. It is for this reason Dimara et al (2020) argues her definition from a comparative basis that aims to gauge how one definition reinforces or pushes an idea.


In their article, Dimara et al (2020) note and reveal what they consider mandatory components of interaction, namely a.) external and internal entities, external and internal actions. What does this mean? Well, central to these components are two objects, a human and a machine (data) this defines what they call external entities. In this case the user is a human being who is characterised by demographics, skills, abilities and motivation. This is essentially the "object" - person - who initiates the interaction to share a moment in time. In contrast Internal entities is interaction characterised by goal orientated activities involving data-orientated intent (Dimara et al 2020). On the other hand, actions are what mediates these two streams of entities by creating a dialogue between the user and the visualisation. The user performs an action on the system and the system returns feeback that is perceived by the user. Whereas internal actions are intangible cognitive acts of the user such as processing the data.


Interaction for visualisation commonly involves all of these components. External entities involve external physical objects such as a mouse, pen, keyboard and physical constraints such as the environment under which interaction occurs. Internal entities uses prior knowledge to establish how a user may act such as externally changing the data with filtering and aggregation techniques. Therefore from these components we can deduce that interaction refers to the action reaction dialogue between the user and the visualisation system whereas Interactivity - which is often used inter changeably refers to the feel, properties and quality of the interaction. I agree with statement because according to (Smuts 2008) for a product to be interactive, something must be responsive in a ways that is neither controllable nor completely random. Therefore, interactivity speaks to the experience of an interaction such as how it controls and challenges the user and not the interaction itself.


That being said there are significant benefits for adding interaction to visualisations as it has been proven in contexts such as games to amplify inactive, human driven data exploration in which the user is in control of the information space. So that the user can have full agency over the affordances provided by the system to act upon their intent. Hence, it is for this reason that according to (Dimara et al 2020) "interaction for visualisation is the interplay between a person and a data interface involving data-related intent".

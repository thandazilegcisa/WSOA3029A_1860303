Artistic Data Visualisation


The use of API's (Application Programming Interfaces) has democratised data and its potential uses. This has had a profound impact on the field of information visualisation. The ability to create visual representations of data on computers and not only reproduce visualisation but expand of the concept has defined this use of data as its own art practice. This is Particularly interesting because data visualisation is meant to tell a story and by adopting this as an artistic practice could engage people in emotional-moral reflection of the information that is presented to them. Which means users are more likely to be persuaded and engaged through such means.


Artistic visualistaions are visualisations of data done by artists with the intent of making art. Intent is very important here because that is the essence of what guides the user as to what storyline is being followed, for example intent also governs the how potential interactions with this webpage might happen - my navigation guides the users intent. Therefore intent gives meaning and contextualises the visualisation of data from a perspective in which the human recognizes the power of visualisation to express a point of view. Hence,is for this reason that visualisations can be used to persuade and to analyse.



Now, because artworks are aesthetic and engross aesthetic pleasures that affect emotional responses. It brings the user-human closer to the reality behind the source of data, therefore encapsulating and affective experience by making the data seemingly tangible. Which is why with this project aims to use storytelling - albeit with text (instead of visual art or auditory art as in my data driven artwork example), data and interaction contexualised through the site.

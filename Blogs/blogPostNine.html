The Weather Score Project


The weather score project is an amalgamation of data driven art work that makes use of not only visuals but auditory compositions of the assimilation of data. It is a project that is entirely based on weather data, which is then adapted by composers to a musical performance. "These musical performances have taken place in a variety of venues from galleries, concert halls to theaters and planetariums".


What is particularly stunning about this project is that there are multiple strategies used in order to create the object. According to Miebach (2011) her she sources data from both primary and secondary sources. Firstly, she gathers data from her own environment without any cost heavy hardware, just common weather measurement units that one can get from a store. Then, she gathers data from secondary sources like satellite readings and then compiles and compares the data set and eventually uses the data to determine the form of her sculpture. On its surface, the sculpture is made up of a vertical and horizontal axis's that can be "tabulated" into different values that pertain to the humidity, temperature, or wind direction.


Miebach not only uses visuals for her data driven art but musical scores which according to her "allows for a more nuanced way of translating information without compromising it". Her saying this is interesting because weather patterns are cyclical patterns that resemble the change in time. And so because audio/and music is temporal. Visual art more so contextualises space & and environments. Therefore, i understand why she would make such a statement.


Whats striking about this work is that it welcomes emotional responses that come from different perspectives. This therefore, welcomes and emphasises critical thinking about weather patterns that comes from an emotional experience of either a.) seeing the project in an art museum as a sculpture or b.) seeing the project in a science museum as a 3D visualisation of data or lastly c.) as a Musical in the form of a musical score. By so doing the project renders weather Visible, Tactile and Musical.

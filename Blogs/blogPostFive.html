Blog Post #5:

Information is Beautiful - What can we do to reduce emissions?


This article examines the representational and communication strategies used in the visualization of the above title. The overall aim of the visualization is to enlighten users with the various ways in which every human on planet earth can play a role in reducing the amount of greenhouse gases causing global warming.


This visualisation takes the style of a visual info-graphic that not only communicates with graphs, but with text and pictographs that make up the iconography of what the text refers to. In this visualisation the colour palette is used to enhance meaning by using colour to either foreground or background specific information on the screen. What is particularly interesting about this visualisation is that it indicates the yearly average of global emissions per person in a particular country. And what this does is that by indicating which persons, from a specific country are close to the current average it examines who - as in which countries citizens - still need to do more in order to fight global warming.


How does this affect my website?


Well, this article provides very valuable insights into what causes global warming, focuses on how an individual can contribute to global warming. This influenced my approach in deciding on i would approach my exam graph. Therefore, in light of becoming conscious of what practices contribute to global warming i wanted measure the air quality of various cities across the world in relation to their population.


This visualisation is very provocative because it is very personal, it does not necessary frame the conversation around countries/cities like my current project, although since i've added a new graph contextualizing the demographics if each city. By so doing it reinforces the intent of this visualisation by framing and putting the onus on an individual to inform themselves about what they can do in their daily lives to reduce co2 emissions. The list includes practices such as, leaving the car at home, instead choosing to take a walk, bike, or train. Taking fewer flights, buying green energy, plant a tree, reduced food wast. By so doing the visualisation not only gives people solutions, but also highlights some of the biggest contributing factors without needing to have an individual graph for such factors.

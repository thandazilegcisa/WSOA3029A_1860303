Blog Post #5:

Information is Beautiful - What can we do to reduce emissions?


This article examines the representational and communication strategies used in the visualisation of the above title. The overall aim of the visualisation is to aquaint users with the various ways in which every human on planet earth can play a role in reducing the amount of greenhouse gases causing global warming.


This visualisation takes the style of a visual infographic that not only communicates with graphs, but with text and pictographs that make up the iconography of what the text refers to. In this visualisation the colour palette is used to enhance meaning by using colour to either foreground or background specific information on the screen. What is particularly interesting about this visualisation is that it indicates the current yearly average of golabl emissions per person in a particular country. And what this does is that by indicating which persons, from a sepcific country are close to the current average it highlights who - which countries citizens - still need to do more in order to fight global warming.


This visualisation is very provocative because it is very personal, it does not necessary frame the conversation around countries/cities like my current project. Instead it puts the onus on an individual by informing readers about what they can do in their daily lives to reduce co2 emissions. This includes things such as, leaving the car at home, instead choosing to take a walk, bike, or train. Taking fewer flights, buying green energy, plant a tree, reduced food wast. By so doing the visualisation not only gives people solutions, but also highlights some of the biggest contributing factors without needing to have an individual graph for such factors.

Information is Beautiful - Gender Pay Gap


In the visualisation of the above title. The authour McCandles (2016) illustrates the gender pay gap between males and females between major geo-political alliances - The UK & US. The graph is a unique scatter plot graph that uses connections between dots - purple for female & green for males - to signify the gap between the two genders.


The interaction in this visualisation allows the user to follow their intent by providing users the option to filter or sort information depending on a specific category. The categories provided include sorting the plot either by salary - which displays values in dollars on the x-axis by an increment of 10 in thousands - or gap, which displays the pay gap in percentages. This was particularly helpful because it made the data very easily readable in terms of the core aim of the visualisation - examining "gender pay gaps". It gave ne the ability to sort the data and understand the core premise of why the visualisation was made.


And because the plot by gap is very readable, it appeals much more to the emotional response one might get by interacting with the data in this way. By so doing it kept me motivated to explore the other options that interaction provided, including sorting the job category on the x-axis by either the default orientation of job categories, or in descending or ascending order. This allowed me to easily be able summarise as to which job occupations have the largest or least pay gap - Legal occupations. This was intresting to find out but yet not suprising cause followed by Legal occupations is Doctors, Chief Executives, Financial Advisors, and Construction workers these jobs have endured privileges of status within communities with children being urged to work in these industries.


Whereas, when i sorted the plot by salaries with the narrowest gap, you learn that occupations with the narrowest gap are the least desired jobs within society like bus driving, social working, security workers,and office clerks. Thus, the sorting interactions afforded us gave me agency to sort of create an interactive narrative driven by my intent. As such by means of these interactions i was able to ultimately conclude that the US particularly has a higher gender pay gap by changing the data set examined to the UK which fairs well in illustrating that women in some sectors of the UK economy earn more than men. This is encouraging and potentially shows which country has better work place policies that consider the needs of women and their role in the economy because not only are there considerably low pay gaps in "discouraged jobs" but also those that have the privilege of status.


References:
